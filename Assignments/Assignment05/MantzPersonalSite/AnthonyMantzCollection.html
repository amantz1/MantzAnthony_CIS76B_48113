Anthony Mantz's Microphone Collection

In 1996, when Anthony Mantz discovered his interest in audio recording, he began started what will become an extensive collection of microphones. The collection began from utilitarian first purchase of a Shure SM57 to use as a very basic, general-purpose mic to record his saxophone. Over time as Anthony began recording other instruments and larger projects, the collection grew to accomodate a wide variety of recording applications. The collection now spans a wide variety of microphones from vintage to modern. His current favorite is a recent aquisition of a 1963 Neumann U67. To quote Candace Stewart at East West Studios in Hollywood, "You have to work real hard to sound bad using this gear."
